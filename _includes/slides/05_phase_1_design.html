Phase 1: Design

Keep It Simple, Stupid

  • Avoid temptation to complicate migration to solve outstanding problems
    • Don't overlook impact of tech debt on speed, ease, and agility
  • Prefer what is simple over what is easy
  • Minimize the number of variables whenever possible
    • Handle upgrades separately (e.g. Cassandra 1.2 -> Cassandra 2.2)
    • Handle internal service substitutions separately (VPC -> stunnel)
    • Handle environment substitutions separately (VM -> Docker)
  • Prefer incremental progress as opposed to depending on completion

Keep A Record What and Why

  • What is obvious today won't be obvious next week (much less next year)
  • The human brain is not deterministic
  • Other teams/engineers coming onto the project will better understand the history of decisions and the impact of those decisions
    • History of the migration is more important than you realize when it comes time to debug
  • Reduce the chance of everyone forgetting some minor but critical detail
  • Things change

Beware egress costs!

  • Limit extraneous egress to save $$$
  • Running some tasks on EC2 compute may be more affordable

Verifying the data

  • Decide how you're going to audit the data up front
  • Don't underestimate the confidence required to actually delete data
  • Likely a task worth running on EC2 compute
  • Reduce how much of the data is audited over time
    • Always audit some of the data so regressions can be detected

Deleting the data

  • Consider relationship between audits and deletions
    • Delete each item as audited?
    • Delete each bucket after fully audited?
  • Prefer strategies that allow you to delete data item by item rather than bucket by bucket, incremental > all at once on completion
  • Don't underestimate the confidence required to actually delete data
  • Figure out what sort of safety window is needed before a delete becomes permanent if you're auditing less than all the data
