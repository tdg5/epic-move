Unsustainable Costs

  • Hundreds of EC2 Instances
    • Typically larger, more expensive instance types
  • 4 petabytes of data in S3 at beginning of project
  • More than 10PB by the time migrations began
  • Obligatory scale comparisons:
    • 10PB == 15.3 million CDs
      • Stacked vertically they'd be more than 11 miles tall
    • 10PB == 2.2 million DVDs
    • If you counted one bit per second,
      it would take almost 3 billion years to count all 10PB

Talking Heads — Once In A Lifetime

How did we get here?

  • Minimizing vendor lock-in has its costs
    • Other than EC2 & S3, minimal dependency on other AWS services
    • Compute and operational knowledge required to roll your own
    • Forget yee not:
      compute == money && operational_knowledge == money
    • Compute tends to stick around longer than operational knowledge

How did we get here?

  • Early assumptions / design decisions didn't work out
    • No process of data de-duplication
    • Dead data most often "deleted" by destroying encryption keys
    • Manual and cumbersome process to delete dead block storage
  • Atypical usage patterns
    • A few outliers can turn a strained situation into a full blown nightmare
    • Under certain circumstances customers could contribute costs at a quadratic rate while we billed them at a constant, or at best, linear rate
